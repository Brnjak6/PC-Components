Brief introduction before start

What should you know before you start building your pc?

Your build should be consisted of mandatory parts and, per your preference, additional parts. Below we’ve mentioned both. Because this is just a brief overview of components, don’t worry about the final solution. That is exactly what we will figure out in the app.

CPU

Calculates everything in your system. Having a good cpu will get you far. Wide range of options available from AMD and Intel

Motherboard

Connects and manages all other components onto itself. Should be careful about compatibility when choosing one

GPU

Comes in two shapes. Integrated and discrete. Processes any visuals and outputs to monitor. No matter your budget, there should be room for a gpu.

RAM

Stores CPU data and reads/writes it very fast on a temporary basis. That means once your PC is shut down all the data is lost. It is also very common to have 2 or more kits. Depending on your needs

HDD/SSD

Retains all used data on our system. Compared to HDD, SSD reads data much faster and is becoming a new standard. Hint: Combination of both is not excluded

PSU

Charges the whole system. Keeping an eye on the ratio of wattage needed and supplied is most important here

Case

Keep it all in one place and clean with more functionalities. Be sure your setup has room to breath in it

CPU Cooler

Your temperatures play important role and keeping them in check is easier with direct cooling on to CPU

Additional parts
Monitor

PC can be output to different displays, although for best performance monitor is recommended.

M&K

Mouse and keyboard are a common purchase with new built, but you might skip this one if you are already equipped

Speakers

Going with monitor means you will be in a need of better speakers, since built in ones are not competitive

DVD/Blu-Ray

You might consider it in case you have physical collection of DVDs ready to be played
